About Us

"Saving lives, serving nations since 1948". WHO is a global health Organization which contributes towards manifesting technical assistance to countries, sets international health standards also it has played a leading role in several public health achievements. Founded in 1948, WHO is the United Nations agency that connects nations, partners and people to promote health, keep the world safe and serve the vulnerable – so everyone, everywhere can attain the highest level of health.

WHO leads global efforts to expand universal health coverage. We direct and coordinate the world’s response to health emergencies. And we promote healthier lives – from pregnancy care through old age. Our Triple Billion targets outline an ambitious plan for the world to achieve good health for all using science-based policies and programmes.

Services

Neurology

The World Health Organization (WHO) actively engages in neurology by providing guidelines and support for the prevention, diagnosis, and management of neurological disorders. It emphasizes research, surveillance, and capacity-building to address conditions such as epilepsy.

Eyes care

WHO prioritizes eye care, aiming to eliminate avoidable blindness through initiatives like Vision 2020. Their Global Action Plan guides countries in providing integrated and accessible eye care, emphasizing early detection and treatment. For details, visit WHO's official resources on eye care.

Pulomonary

The World Health Organization (WHO) focuses on pulmonary health by offering guidance on the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of respiratory conditions. It addresses issues like asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and respiratory infections.

Cardiology

WHO guides global cardiology efforts, focusing on policy development, prevention, and treatment strategies. Emphasis lies on tackling cardiovascular diseases through lifestyle promotion, risk factor control, and support for comprehensive health programs worldwide.

Dental Care

WHO prioritizes dental care as a crucial component of global health. Their initiatives emphasize oral health education, and integrating dental services into primary healthcare. The goal is to reduce oral diseases and ensure equitable access to quality dental care worldwide.

Hepatology

WHO focuses on hepatology care, emphasizing strategies for the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of viral hepatitis (B and C). Initiatives include vaccination, improved testing, and comprehensive public health measures to reduce the global burden of liver diseases.
